## 1. Purpose
This playbook provides operational guidance for responding to suspected or confirmed account compromise incidents affecting Burn and Churn Coffee systems and personnel.

Account compromise incidents occur when unauthorized individuals gain access to company accounts through methods such as phishing, credential theft, password reuse, or malicious software.

This playbook ensures that compromised accounts are identified quickly, contained effectively, and restored safely in order to protect business operations and sensitive information.

----

## 2. Scope
This playbook applies to account-compromised incidents affecting the following systems:
  - Google Workspace user accounts
  - Google Workspace administrative accounts
  - Toast POS administrative access
  - Company-issued tablets connected to company accounts
  - Network administration accounts

All employees, managers, and authorized IT support vendors must follow this playbook when responding to suspected or confirmed account compromised incidents.

-------
## 3. Indicators of an Account Compromise
Account compromise incidents may be suspected when one or more of the following indicators are observed:
  - Unusual login alerts or unfamiliar login locations
  - Unexpected password reset notifications
  - Emails sent from an employee account without their knowledge
  - Unauthorized changes to account settings or permissions
  - Creation of suspicious mailbox forwarding rules
  - Suspicious administrative activity in Google Workspace
  - Reports of fraudulent activity or unauthorized transactions
  - Alerts generated from security monitoring tools

Employees should report suspicious account activity immediately to the **Store Manager or Business Owner**.

----
## Roles and Responsibilities
### Business Owner
Responsible for overall incidents oversight and escalation decisions, including:
  - Approving major containment or remediation actions
  - Coordinating with external vendors or service providers if necessary
  - Determining whether external notifications are required
  - Reviewing incident documentation and approving improvements

---
### Store Manager
Serves as the primary incident response coordinator and is responsible for:
  - Receiving reports of suspicious account activity
  - Conducting initial incident assessment
  - Initiating containment actions
  - Coordinating response actions with IT vendors if necessary
  - Escalating significant incidents to the Business Owner

----
### Employees
Employees must:
  - Report suspicious account activity immediately
  - Avoid interacting with suspicious messages or links
  - Follow the response instructions provided by management

----
### IT vendors
If technical assistance is required, authorized IT vendors may assist with:
  - Reviewing login activity and system logs
  - Investigating potential unauthorized access
  - Implementing remediation actions
  - Supporting system recovery activities

----
## 5. Response Procedure
### Step 1: Detection and Reporting
  - Suspicious account activity is identified through employee reporting, login alerts, or system monitoring.
  -  The employee must report the activity immediately to the **Store Manager or Business Owner**.
  -  The Store Manager begins initial incident evaluation.

----
### Step 2: Initial Assessment
The Store Manager or Business Owner should determine:
  - Whether the account shows signs of unauthorized access
  - Whether the user recently interacted with a phishing message
  - Whether unauthorized emails or actions occurred
  - Whether administrative privileges may have been affected

If compromise is suspected, proceed immediately to containment actions.

----
### Step 3: Containment
Containment actions may include:
  - Resetting the affected user's password immediately
  - Revoking active login sessions for the account
  - Temporarily disabling the compromised account if necessary
  - Enforcing or verifying multi-factor authentication (MFA)
  - Removing suspicious mailbox rules or email forwarding settings
  - Reviewing administrative privileges associated with the account

If multiple accounts appear affected, escalate the incident to the **Business Owner** immediately.

----
### Step 4: Investigation
Management or IT support should review available logs and system information, including:
  - Recent login activity in Google Workspace
  - Login locations and device information
  - Email activity or unusual sent messages
  - Administrative actions performed by the account
  - Any related suspicious activity affecting other accounts

If additional compromised accounts are identified, containment actions should be expanded accordingly.

----
### Step 5: Eradication and Remediation
Remediation steps may include: 
  - Removing malicious email rules or unauthorized account settings
  - Resetting passwords for affected accounts
  - Ensuring MFA is enabled for affected accounts
  - Updating security configurations if vulnerabilities are identified
  - Reviewing employee security practices if phishing was involved

----
### Step 6: Recovery
Recovery activities include:
  - Restoring secure access to the affected account
  - Confirming the account is functioning normally
  - Monitoring account activity for suspicious behavior following recovery
  - Verifying that no unauthorized changes remain within the account

----
## 6. Communication
Internal communication regarding the incident should be limited to: 
  - Business Owner
  - Store Manager
  - Employees involved in the incident
  - Authorized IT support vendors
External communication must be approved by the **Business Owner** before being shared with vendors, customers, or third parties.

----
## 7. Documentation
The following information should be documented for account-compromised incidents:
  - Date and time the incident was detected
  - The individual reporting the incident
  - Description of the suspicious activity
  - Account(s) affected
  - Actions taken to contain the incident
  - Investigation findings
  - Final resolution and remediation actions

Documentation should be stored with other incident response records.

----
## 8. Lessons Learned
Following confirmed account compromised incidents, management should review:
  - How the compromise occurred
  - Whether existing security controls were effective
  - Whether additional employee security training is required
  - Whether improvements to authentication or monitoring controls are needed

Improvements should be incorporated into the security program where appropriate.
