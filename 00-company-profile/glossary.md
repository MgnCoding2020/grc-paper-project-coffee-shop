# Glossary

## Access Control (AC)
The process of ensuring that only authorized users can access systems and data based on their role and responsibilities. Access control is implemented through account management, authentication, and permission enforcement.

---

## Authentication
The process of verifying the identity of a user or system before granting access. This may include passwords, multi-factor authentication, or other verification methods.

---

## Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
A security mechanism requiring users to provide two or more forms of authentication to verify their identity, reducing the risk of unauthorized access.

---

## Least Privilege
The principle of granting users only the minimum level of access necessary to perform their job functions. This reduces the risk of misuse or unauthorized activity.

---

## Control
A safeguard or countermeasure implemented to reduce risk. Controls may be technical, administrative, or procedural and are mapped to recognized frameworks such as NIST SP 800-53.

---

## Control Matrix
A structured mapping of risks to controls, including control descriptions, implementation status, and supporting evidence. It serves as a central reference for evaluating the security posture of the environment.

---

## Risk
The potential for a threat to exploit a vulnerability, resulting in harm to systems, data, or business operations. Risks are evaluated based on likelihood and impact.

---

## Risk Register
A document used to identify, assess, and track risks within the environment. It includes inherent risk, associated controls, and residual risk after mitigation.

---

## Residual Risk
The level of risk that remains after controls have been implemented. Residual risk reflects the effectiveness and maturity of existing controls.

---

## Evidence
Documentation or artifacts that demonstrate whether a control is implemented and operating as expected. Examples include logs, reports, and system exports.

---

## Evidence Matrix
A mapping of controls to supporting evidence artifacts and validation methods. It helps demonstrate traceability between control implementation and verification.

---

## POA&M (Plan of Action & Milestones)
A document used to track identified control gaps, weaknesses, and remediation efforts. It outlines planned actions, ownership, and status of corrective activities.

---

## Incident Response
The structured process of identifying, containing, investigating, and resolving security incidents. Incident response activities are guided by playbooks and documented for review.

---

## Incident Playbook
A predefined set of steps used to respond to specific types of security incidents, such as phishing or account compromise. Playbooks ensure consistent and effective response actions.

---

## Log Review
The process of reviewing system and activity logs to identify unusual behavior, potential security incidents, or operational issues. Log review supports early detection and response.

---

## Network Segmentation
The practice of separating network components (such as POS systems and business Wi-Fi) to reduce exposure and limit the spread of potential threats.

---

## POS (Point of Sale)
The Toast POS system used to process customer transactions. It is a critical system within the environment and is subject to access control and monitoring.

---

## Vendor / Service Provider
A third-party organization that provides services supporting business operations (e.g., Toast POS, Google Workspace). Vendor risk must be managed due to limited control over external systems.

---

## Payment Processor
A third-party service responsible for securely processing payment transactions. The business relies on the processor to handle sensitive payment data rather than storing it locally.

---

## System Boundary
The defined scope of systems, users, and services included in the environment. The system boundary determines which components are subject to security controls and monitoring.

---

## Traceability
The ability to link risks, controls, evidence, validation activities, and remediation efforts. Traceability ensures consistency and supports audit readiness.

---

## Control Validation
The process of verifying that controls are implemented and functioning as intended. In this project, validation is supported through evidence review and lightweight automation scripts.
