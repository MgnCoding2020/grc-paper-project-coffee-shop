# Payment Card Data Protection Policy
Burn and Churn Coffee

## Document Control
**Policy Owner:** Business Owner | 
**Approved by:** Business Owner | 
**Effective Date:** March 2026 | 
**Review Frequency:** Annual or upon changes to payment processing systems | 
**Version:** 1.0

----------

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish security requirements for protecting payment card information processed by Burn and Churn Coffee.

This policy supports the protection of customer payment information and aligns with the security principles outlined in the **Payment card Indusrty Data Security Standard (PCI DSS)

Burn and Churn Coffee processes payment card transactions through a third-party payment processor (Toast POS). While the payment processor managers many technical PCI controls, Burn and Churn Coffee
remains responsible for protecting payment systems, devices, and access to payment environments. 

This policy aims to:
  - Prevent unauthorized access to payment card data
  - Protect point-of-sale (POS) systems from misuse or tampering
  - Ensure payment processing is performed only through approved systems
  - Reduce financial and reputational risk associated with payment fraud

-----------

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to:
  - Business Owned
  - Store Managers
  - Shift Leaders
  - Employees
  - Authorized third-party service providers

Systems and devices in scope include:
  - Toast POS terminals
  - POS administrative portal
  - Company-issued tablets used for order processing
  - Network infrastructure supporting POS connectivity
  - Payment processing integrations provided by Toast
    
This policy applies to **all payment card transactions** processed by Burn and Churn Coffee.

----------------------

## 3. Payment Card Data Handling
Burn and Churn Coffee **does not store payment card numbers locally** and relies on the Toast POS system for secure payment processing.

Employees shall:
  - Process all payment cards using the approved Toast POS system.
  - Ensure payment cards are handled only during legitimate transactions.
  - Protect customer privacy during payment transactions.
    
Employees shall not:
  - Write down payment card numbers
  - Store card numbers in notebooks, spreadsheets, or local files
  - Photograph or copy payment card information
  - Request full card numbers through email or messaging systems
  - Bypass the approved payment system
    
If payment card data is accidentally exposed or recorded, the incident must be reported immediately under the **Incident Response Policy**.

----------------------

## 4. Point-of-Sale (POS) Security
POS systems are critical business systems and must be protected from misuse or tampering.

The following controls shall be maintained:
  - Only authorized personnel may access the POS administrative portal.
  - POS terminals shall remain visible to staff during business hours.
  - Employees must report any signs of device tampering immediately.
  - POS devices must not be modified, opened, or reconfigured by employees.
    
Examples of suspicious activity include:
  - Unrecognized devices attached to POS terminals
  - Broken or altered seals on devices
  - Unexpected prompts or behavior on the POS system
    
Any suspected tampering must be treated as a **security incident**.

---------------------------------

## 5. Device and Physical Security
POS terminals and company-issued tablets shall remain under employee supervision during store operations.

Employees shall not:
  - Connect unauthorized USB devices to POS terminals
  - Install software on POS devices
  - Remove POS devices from their designated location without authorization
    
Because Burn and Churn Coffee operates within a leased facility, employees should remain attentive to unauthorized access to areas where POS equipment is located.

Any suspicious activity involving equipment should be reported to the Store Manager immediately.

---------------

## 6. Vendor and Payment Processor Responsibility
Burn and Churn relies on **Toast POS** as its primary payment processor.

Toast is responsible for:
  - Payment card processing infrastructure
  - Encryption of payment data
  - Compliance with applicable PCI DSS technical requirements

Burn and Churn Coffee remains responsible for:
  - Securig physical POS devices
  - Restricting administrative access
  - Ensuring employees follow secure payment handling practices
  - Reporting suspected security incidents involving payment systems

--------------------------------------

## 7. Monitoring and Reporting
Employees must immediately report any suspicious activity involving payment processing systems, including:
  - Unusual POS behavior
  - Unauthorized refunds or financial discrepancies
  - Signs of device tampering
  - Customer reports of suspicious charges

Reports shall be made to the **Store Manager or Business Owner**.

Payment-related incidents shall be handled in accordance with the **Incident Response Policy**.

--------------------------------------------

## 8. PCI DSS Awareness
Burn and Churn Coffee acknowledges its responsibility to protect payment card information in accordance with applicable PCI DSS guidelines.

Security practices within this policy support PCI DSS principles, including:
  - Restricting access to payment systems
  - Protecting physical payment devices
  - Monitoring for suspicious activity
  - responding to potential security incidents

Where required, the **Business Owner** may complete PCI Self-Assessment Questionnaire (SAQ) documentation with the payment processor.

---------------------------------------------

## 9. Enforement
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action in accordance with company management procedures, up to and including termination of employment or contractual relationship.
